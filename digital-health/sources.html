Works Cited

All claims made across this website are supported by credible, peer-reviewed, or government-published sources. Full references are listed below.

Primary Sources

The following sources were used as the primary research base for this website. Sources include peer-reviewed journal articles, Statistics Canada publications, and resources from recognised health organisations.

  1. Bashir, H., & Bhat, S. A. (2017). "Effects of Social Media on Mental Health: A Review." International Journal of Indian Psychology, 4(3). Retrieved via PubMed Central (PMC), National Institutes of Health.

    https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10852174/
    Peer-Reviewed Journal

  2. Statistics Canada. (2024). "Screen time and mental health of Canadians." Statistics Canada Catalogue no. 22-20-0001. Government of Canada.

    https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/22-20-0001/222000012024001-eng.htm
    Government Publication

  3. MedlinePlus / U.S. National Library of Medicine. (2024). "How to Improve Mental Health." MedlinePlus Health Information. U.S. National Institutes of Health.

    https://medlineplus.gov/howtoimprovementalhealth.html
    Government Health Authority

  4. Orlowski, J. (Director). (2020). The Social Dilemma [Documentary film]. Exposure Labs / Netflix. Interviews with former technology executives including Tristan Harris (former Design Ethicist at Google) on the intentional addictiveness of social media platforms.

    Official Trailer: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uaaC57tcci0
    Documentary Film

  5. Twenge, J. M., Joiner, T. E., Rogers, M. L., & Martin, G. N. (2018). "Increases in Depressive Symptoms, Suicide-Related Outcomes, and Suicide Rates Among U.S. Adolescents After 2010 and Links to Increased New Media Screen Time." Clinical Psychological Science, 6(1), 3–17. SAGE Journals / Association for Psychological Science.

    https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2167702617723376
    Peer-Reviewed Journal

  6. Hunt, M. G., Marx, R., Lipson, C., & Young, J. (2018). "No More FOMO: Limiting Social Media Decreases Loneliness and Depression." Journal of Social and Clinical Psychology, 37(10), 751–768.

    https://guilfordjournals.com/doi/10.1521/jscp.2018.37.10.751
    Peer-Reviewed Journal

  7. Canadian Paediatric Society, Digital Health Task Force. (2019). "Screen time and young children: Promoting health and development in a digital world." Paediatrics & Child Health, 22(8), 461–468.

    https://cps.ca/en/documents/position/screen-time-and-young-children
    Medical Organisation

  8. Centre for Humane Technology. (2022). Ledger of Harms: The documented harms of social media and technology on people and society. Humane Technology Research Foundation.

    https://ledger.humanetech.com/
    Research Foundation

  9. American Academy of Ophthalmology. (2023). "Computers, Digital Devices and Eye Strain." Eye Health Information. aao.org.

    https://www.aao.org/eye-health/tips-prevention/computer-usage
    Medical Organisation

  10. Kids Help Phone. (2024). About Kids Help Phone: Canada's Only 24/7 National Support Service for Young People. Toronto: Kids Help Phone.

    https://kidshelpphone.ca/about-us/
